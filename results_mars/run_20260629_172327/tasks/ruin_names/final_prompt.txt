Given an artist, band, or movie name, generate the single most humorous one-character edit that meaningfully transforms the original name. Follow this process:

**Step 1: Identify the core semantic element**
- Determine which character or word in the name carries the most meaning
- Focus on elements that, when changed, would create the biggest conceptual shift

**Step 2: Generate and evaluate potential edits**
Create 3-5 single-character edits (substitution, addition, or deletion) and quickly assess each based on:
- **Humor Potential (Primary Criterion)**: Would it make people laugh due to clever wordplay, irony, or absurdity? Prioritize edits that create immediate amusement.
- **Meaningful Transformation**: Does it create a clear, contrasting meaning that enhances the humor?
- **Recognizability**: Can people still connect it to the original while appreciating the new humorous meaning?

**Step 3: Select the best option**
Choose the edit that:
- **First and foremost**, creates the most surprising and amusing reinterpretation
- Makes people smile or laugh instantly when they see the connection
- Maintains a clear connection to the original
- Avoids edits that are technically correct but not genuinely funny

**Humor Evaluation Check**: Before final selection, ask: "Is this edit actually funny, or just technically clever?" If it doesn't provoke genuine amusement, discard it.

**Output format**:  
Original: [name]  
Edit: [selected one-character edit]  
Why it's funny: [Brief, clear explanation of the humor]

**Key focus**: Humor is the primary driver. Technical perfection is secondary. The best edits should make people laugh or smile immediately upon reading them.

**Optimized Prompt: Dynamic Implicit Transition Detection & Flexible Availability Assessment with Contextual Intensity Resolution**

**Core Objective:** Identify periods where a person could realistically initiate AND complete a meaningful additional activity, with dynamic detection of implicit transitions and calibrated availability thresholds to minimize both false positives and false negatives.

**Enhanced Framework:**
1. **Dynamic Timeline Construction** - Map explicit activities and context-sensitive implicit transitions
2. **Contextual Intensity Resolution Protocol** - Systematically resolve ambiguous or overlapping activity intensities
3. **Calibrated Transition Detection** - Apply flexible duration ranges based on resolved intensity levels
4. **Tiered Availability Assessment** - Classify free time by usability level and minimum viable duration
5. **Activity-Specific Validation** - Match potential free periods to appropriate activity types

**Critical Enhancement: Contextual Intensity Resolution Protocol**

**Step 1: Multi-Dimensional Intensity Assessment**
- **Primary Intensity Indicators:**
  - Cognitive Demand (Low/Medium/High)
  - Physical Exertion (Low/Medium/High)
  - Emotional Load (Low/Medium/High)
  - Time Sensitivity (Flexible/Moderate/Critical)

- **Intensity Resolution Hierarchy:**
  1. **Explicit Context Clues:** "urgent," "intense," "casual," "routine"
  2. **Duration Patterns:** Short durations suggest higher intensity; longer durations suggest sustained engagement
  3. **Adjacency Effects:** High-intensity activities create longer transition buffers
  4. **Default Fallbacks:** Unclear activities default to medium intensity with conservative transitions

**Step 2: Ambiguity Resolution Principles**
- **Conservative Bias:** When intensity is ambiguous, apply higher transition buffers to prevent false positives
- **Pattern Consistency:** Maintain consistent intensity classification across similar activity types
- **Proximity Averaging:** For overlapping intensities, average adjacent activity demands
- **Critical Path Priority:** Prioritize accuracy around high-stakes activities

**Step 3: Practical Simplification Trade-offs**

**Balancing Complexity vs. Accuracy:**
- **Tiered System Benefits:** Granular classification enables precise activity matching
- **Simplification Needs:** Reduce cognitive load for users and computational complexity
- **Balanced Approach:** Maintain three-tier system but with clear, memorable thresholds

**Practical Implementation Compromises:**
- **Fixed Tier Thresholds:** Maintain 10/20/30-minute boundaries for consistency
- **Binary Intensity Classification:** Use Low/High with Medium as default
- **Location & Resource Assumptions:** Assume availability unless explicitly contradicted

**Enhanced Transition Calibration Protocol**

**Dynamic Duration Ranges with Simplified Application:**
- **Low Intensity:** 5-15 minutes (routine, casual, familiar activities)
- **Medium Intensity:** 10-20 minutes (default for ambiguous cases)
- **High Intensity:** 15-30 minutes (stressful, critical, unfamiliar activities)

**Context-Sensitive Override Rules:**
- **Sequential High-Intensity:** Add 5-minute cumulative fatigue buffer
- **Mixed Intensity Sequences:** Use the higher intensity for transition calculation
- **Routine Patterns:** Reduce transitions by 25% for repeated similar activities

**Tiered Free Time Classification with Practical Optimization**

**Availability Tiers (Simplified for Real-World Use):**
- **Tier 1: Substantial Free Time** (≥30 minutes)
  - Suitable for meaningful, focused activities
  - Minimal adjacent transitions or context switching

- **Tier 2: Useful Free Time** (15-29 minutes)
  - Good for moderate tasks with some flexibility
  - May require minor preparation or cleanup

- **Tier 3: Brief Opportunities** (10-14 minutes)
  - Limited to quick, low-cognitive tasks
  - Must account for immediate next activities

**Balanced Decision Protocol with Practical Constraints**

**False Positive Prevention (Conservative):**
- Never identify free time within 10 minutes of explicitly time-sensitive activities
- Require minimum 5-minute buffer after any high-intensity marker
- Assume location changes require 15+ minutes unless evidence suggests otherwise

**False Negative Prevention (Practical):**
- Identify all gaps ≥10 minutes as potentially usable
- Allow multi-tasking during "preparation" periods for routine activities
- Recognize that some transitions can be compressed for familiar routines

**Enhanced Example Demonstration with Ambiguity Resolution:**

**Input:** "Woke up at 7:00, quick shower, important breakfast meeting, left for work at 8:30, team strategy session 9:00-10:30, worked until lunch at 12:00, ate for 30 minutes, critical client call 1:00-1:45, finished work at 5:00"

**Intensity Resolution Processing:**
- "Quick shower" → Low intensity (routine, familiar)
- "Important breakfast meeting" → High intensity (important, likely focused)
- "Team strategy session" → Medium-High intensity (collaborative but strategic)
- "Critical client call" → High intensity (critical, high-stakes)

**Dynamic Processing with Resolved Intensities:**
1. **Calibrated Timeline:**
   - 7:00-7:15: Quick shower (15min, Low intensity)
   - 7:15-7:45: Important breakfast meeting (30min, High intensity)
   - 7:45-8:15: **Implicit: Preparation** (20-30min range → 25min applied, High intensity carryover)
   - 8:15-8:45: **Implicit: Commute** (25-35min range → 30min applied)
   - 8:45-9:00: **Tier 3 free time** (15min) - Quick tasks only
   - 9:00-10:30: Team strategy session (Medium-High → High applied)
   - 10:30-10:45: **Implicit: Recovery** (10-20min range → 15min applied)
   - 10:45-12:00: Work (Medium intensity default)
   - 12:00-12:30: Lunch
   - 12:30-12:40: **Implicit: Transition** (5-10min range → 8min applied)
   - 12:40-1:00: **Tier 2 free time** (20min) - Moderate tasks possible
   - 1:00-1:45: Critical client call (High intensity)
   - 1:45-2:00: **Implicit: Recovery** (10-20min range → 15min applied)
   - 2:00-5:00: Work

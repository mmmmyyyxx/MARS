Given a sentence with an ambiguous pronoun and multiple-choice options, determine the pronoun's antecedent or identify ambiguity by following these steps:

1. **Identify possible antecedents**—consider nouns in the sentence that match the pronoun in number and gender.
2. **Check for the most logical referent** based on common sense and typical usage—e.g., pronouns often refer to the subject or a recently mentioned noun.
3. **If one antecedent is clearly more reasonable**, select that option. If two or more are equally plausible, choose "Ambiguous."

**Output format**:
- Start with the option letter (e.g., "(A)").
- Briefly justify your choice in one sentence.

Example:
Sentence: *My parent called their secretary for more information*
- "Their" could refer to "parent" or "secretary," and neither is clearly favored.
- **Output**: (C) Ambiguous, because it's unclear whether "their" refers to the parent or the secretary.

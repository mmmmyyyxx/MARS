Given a sentence with an ambiguous pronoun, carefully analyze the context and available information to determine the antecedent of the pronoun. If the pronoun's referent cannot be unambiguously inferred from the given sentence, state that it is ambiguous. Otherwise, identify the specific noun to which the pronoun refers.

Follow these steps:
1. Identify the pronoun in the sentence.
2. Examine the surrounding context and preceding nouns that could serve as potential antecedents.
3. Evaluate grammatical and logical coherence to determine the most plausible antecedent.
4. If multiple antecedents are equally plausible based on the given information, conclude that the sentence is ambiguous.
5. If a clear antecedent can be deduced, state it explicitly.

For example:
Sentence: My parent called their secretary for more information.
Analysis: The pronoun "their" could refer to "my parent" (possessive) or potentially to the secretary if additional context were present. However, based on standard possessive usage and the absence of contradictory information, "their" most logically refers to "my parent." Therefore, the antecedent is "my parent."
